# Animated Object Parent Evaluation

Creature: Animated object, Bestiary 1 page 14

Reference-derived target:
- A medium animated cage, not a generic animated object.
- Dead skeleton locked inside and rattling as the cage moves.
- Domed cage body made from aged metal and wood.
- Spiked curved ribs/bars around the cage.
- Chain legs and arms ending in claw-like hooks.
- Lurching construct posture.

## Production Lane Decisions

Selected production set:
- `animated-object-bestiary-light-cage-skeleton.png`
- `animated-object-silhouette-light-chain-hooks.png`
- `animated-object-bestiary-dark-spiked-cage.png`
- `animated-object-crpg-dark-lurching-cage.png`

Evaluation:
- The four production lanes all preserve the key Pathfinder identity: animated cage body, visible trapped skeleton, spiked bars, chain limbs, and hook-claw ends.
- None of the selected lanes turn the creature into a living flesh monster, a robot, a simple empty cage, or a humanoid wearing cage armor.
- The light bestiary lane is the clearest general-purpose reference image.
- The light silhouette lane is the strongest thumbnail/game-read option.
- The dark bestiary lane is the cleanest dark-theme static asset.
- The CRPG dark lane is the most dynamic and encounter-ready; it has tighter framing, but the hooks remain visible and the cage silhouette is intact.

Residual risks:
- Some hook hardware in the silhouette and CRPG lanes has a polished mechanical finish, but the designs still read as forged chain-and-cage constructs rather than sci-fi robots.
- The alternates are preserved for audit and comparison, but the four named production lanes should be preferred for downstream use.

Final status: production-ready for the requested four-lane Animated object set.
